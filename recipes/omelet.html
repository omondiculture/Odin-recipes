How to make a French Omelet

Home

Description

This is a recipe of how to make a perfect french omelet.The above photo shows my omelet after i finished the steps I'm going to outline here.Make sure you follow along for perfect results and perfect food .

Omelet making is a skill that takes time, patience, gentle touch and a whole lot off eggs and butter.The absolute perfect French omelet should be smooth and pale(there should never be any browning) on the outside and creamy, curdy, and custardy on the inside.With that being said, even omelets that are just shy of perfect are incredibly delicious, perhaps just a bit rough around the edges.

Ingredients

  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter
  • 1 pinch kosher salt
  • 1 pinch white pepper
  • 1 tablespoon finely chopped fresh herbs(chives, parsley, and tarrragon)

Steps

Ready to give it a whirl? Here's how it's done:

  1. Whisk Your Eggs Grab the finest eggs you can find and whisk them together.Typically, for a medium to large skillet, one omelet is 3 large eggs. It's important that these eggs are whisked vigorously for at least 30 seconds to ensure that the whites and yolks are fully incorporated. Season the eggs with a pinch of salt and freshly ground black pepper after there are no streaks of white and it is one steady, uniform stream of liquid.
  2. Melt The butter You always want to use a non-stick pan for an omelet.Set it over medium-low heat and slowly melt 1 tablespoon of butter. Once the butter is melted, swirl it around in the pan so that the entire bottom is coated.
  3. Add The Eggs and Stir When the eggs are added into the butter skillet, you should not hear a sizzling sound. If you do, your pan is too hot and you should turn down the heat.Once the eggs are in, use a rubber spatula to mix them around in the pan, while using your other hand to move the skillet in a small, circular motion.
  4. Let It Set Once you start to see small, fluffy curds forming, it's time to let your omelet rest over a very low heat.The eggs should set just enough so you have something to peel back when you touch the edges with your rubber spatula.
  5. Add Your Fillings(Optional) A traditional French omelet is just eggs,butter and salt, but if you want to jazz up your omelet with some fillings,now is the time to do so.Add any cooked meat or crumble in some goat cheese.If you want to add veggies, add a scant layer of sauteed mushrooms, peppers, or spinach.Just make sure whatever filling you add is already cooked.
  6. Roll It Up
  7. Plate and Garnish
